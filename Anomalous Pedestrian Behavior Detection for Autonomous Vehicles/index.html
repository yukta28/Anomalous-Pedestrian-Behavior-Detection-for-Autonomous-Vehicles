Anomalous Pedestrian Behavior Detection for Autonomous Vehicles

Introduction

The development of autonomous vehicles requires robust perception systems capable of accurately detecting pedestrians and ensuring safety in urban environments. However, pedestrian behavior is highly unpredictable, with actions such as jaywalking, sudden stops, and erratic movements posing significant risks. This project focuses on detecting anomalous pedestrian behavior using deep learning techniques, particularly YOLOv8 for object detection and an LSTM Autoencoder for anomaly detection.

Data Collection & Preprocessing

The dataset used for this project is the JAAD (Joint Attention for Autonomous Driving) dataset, which contains real-world urban driving scenarios captured from vehicle dashboards. The raw video data was processed by extracting frames and applying the YOLOv8 model to detect pedestrians, extracting their bounding box coordinates. These detections were then stored for motion analysis.

Anomaly Detection Approach

To identify anomalous behavior, we computed velocity and acceleration for each detected pedestrian based on frame-to-frame movement. An LSTM Autoencoder was trained on normal pedestrian movements and used to detect deviations from expected behavior by analyzing reconstruction errors. Higher reconstruction errors indicate anomalous behavior such as sudden movement changes, erratic walking, or unsafe pedestrian activity.

Visualizations & Results

To better understand pedestrian behavior, several visualizations were generated:

  • Heatmap of Pedestrian Movements: Highlights common walking areas and high-risk zones.
  • Velocity vs. Acceleration Scatter Plot: Shows speed variations and potential anomalies.
  • Trajectory Plot: Displays pedestrian movement paths over time.
  • Anomalous Frames: Highlights frames with unusual pedestrian behavior.

Conclusion & Future Work

This project successfully demonstrated the effectiveness of deep learning-based anomaly detection for pedestrian safety in autonomous driving environments. The integration of YOLOv8 and LSTM Autoencoder allowed us to detect irregular pedestrian behavior with high accuracy. Future improvements include deploying the model in real-time AV systems, improving dataset diversity, and refining the anomaly thresholding technique to minimize false positives.
